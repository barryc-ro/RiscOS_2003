LinkUp Systems and ISDCorp. Announce Availability of VxWorks® for the L7200 System Development Board

Companies Deliver System on Chip Solution with True Real Time Operating System Performance for Internet Appliances, Consumer Electronics

SANTA CLARA, Calif. - Sept 25, 2000 - LinkUp Systems Corp., provider of processor plus peripherals solutions for Internet appliances and consumer electronics, and Integrated Software & Devices Corporation (ISDCorp) a leading supplier of system software, intellectual property and software services for the embedded computing market, today announced the availability of VxWorks® support for the LinkUp L7200 system development board.

LinkUp Systems, an ARM Partnership member, supplies the L7200 processor plus peripherals family designed for creating Internet appliances such as Pocket PCs, wireless Internet terminals, smart cellular phones and other embedded products. LinkUp's L7200 family includes an ARM720Tä CPU core, an integrated digital signal processor (DSP), and many key peripherals such as SDRAM and LCD controllers, FLASH/SRAM/ROM memory, smart card and Multimedia card controllers, a DMA controller and several serial interfaces, all on a single chip.

ISDCorp. has over nine years of experience supporting electronics OEMs with operating system ports and device drivers for their embedded systems.

Together, the two companies offer a complete development platform for Internet appliance and consumer electronics OEMs, including processor, peripheral devices and Wind River's VxWorks®, a widely-adopted real time operating system (RTOS).

"As an experienced Wind River WindLink™ Partner, ISDCorp. has proved an ideal development partner for LinkUp Systems in porting the VxWorks RTOS to the L7200 platform," said Ravi Bhatnagar, President and CEO LinkUp Systems. "Together, our companies can provide designers of Internet appliances, consumer electronics and embedded devices with an embedded real time solution based on the highly-integrated L7200 System-on-Chip solution."

"We are pleased to partner with LinkUp Systems, a leading supplier of System-on-Silicon solutions, in providing the VxWorks development environment for the L7200 processor," said Dan Wald, Vice President of Business Development for ISDCorp. "Our companies are providing a high performance embedded solution that will enable our OEM customers to create innovative products and achieve a fast time to market."

LinkUp Systems provides reference designs, development system support and an integrated debug environment for its products. The development system consists of the L7200SDB development board with its associated software, such as support for low-level driver APIs (L7200API) in source form. A complete software toolkit (L7200TLK), optimized for the L7200 on-chip DSP co-processor, is also available. The debug environment consists of an ARM debug monitor and Multi-ICE* In-Circuit Emulator through a JTAG interface.

About ISDCorp
